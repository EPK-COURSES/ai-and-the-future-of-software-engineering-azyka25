# Personal Reflection

While researching discussions in the software engineering community, I realized how much artificial intelligence is changing software development. Before researching this topic, I mostly thought AI was simply another programming tool, but now I understand how strongly it is affecting the future of software engineering careers and development practices.

What surprised me the most was how divided developers are about AI. Some engineers believe AI will significantly improve productivity and make programming easier, while others are worried about software quality, technical debt, and job opportunities for junior developers.

One concern I have is that developers may become too dependent on AI-generated code without fully understanding it. I think programming still requires strong problem-solving skills and logical thinking. If developers only copy AI-generated solutions, they may struggle when debugging complex systems or solving unique problems.

I also found the discussion about junior developers very interesting. I understand why many people worry that beginners may have fewer opportunities if AI automates simple programming tasks. At the same time, I believe AI can also become a useful learning tool for students if used responsibly.

This research changed my opinion about AI replacing programmers. I now think AI will probably assist developers more than replace them completely. Human creativity, teamwork, communication, and decision-making are still very important in software engineering.

Personally, I think future software engineers must continue learning and adapting to new technologies. I want to improve both my programming skills and my ability to use AI tools responsibly. Developers who combine technical knowledge with creativity and critical thinking will likely remain valuable in the future.
